Jamshetji J. Tata

Jamshaid Tata (March 3, 1839)

Early Life

Jamshetji Jehangir Tata, born on March 3, 1839, in Navsari, Gujarat, India, was a visionary industrialist and philanthropist. He hailed from a prominent Parsi family and received his early education in Bombay (now Mumbai).

Career

Jamshetji Tata played a pivotal role in the industrialization of India during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. He founded the Tata Group, a conglomerate of companies that became one of India's most influential business empires. His notable achievements include:

  • Establishing India's first steel plant, Tata Iron and Steel Company (TISCO), in 1907, which later became Tata Steel.
  • Setting up the Tata Electric Company (now Tata Power) in 1911, contributing to the development of India's power sector.
  • Founding the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) in Bangalore in 1909, a premier research institution.

Death

Jamshetji J. Tata passed away on May 19, 1904, leaving behind a lasting legacy that continues to shape India's industrial and educational landscape.
